🧲 Magnets P3 Diversity Interactions

MOE Learning Objective: Identify magnetic and non-magnetic materials; understand that magnets have two poles (North and South) that attract or repel.

🎯 By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:

  • Test objects to see if they are magnetic
  • Identify the North and South poles of a magnet
  • Predict whether magnets will attract or repel
  • Give examples of how magnets are used in daily life
🇸🇬 Singapore Context: Magnets are used in MRT train doors, refrigerator seals in HDB kitchens, and in the compasses used for orienteering at parks like MacRitchie Reservoir!
💡 Fun Fact: The Earth itself is a giant magnet! That's why a compass needle always points North.

🧠 Check Your Understanding

1. Which of these objects will a magnet attract? MCQ

2. Jamie has two bar magnets. When she brings the North pole of one magnet close to the North pole of another magnet, they push away from each other. Explain why. CER

CER Tip:
• Claim: State your answer clearly
• Evidence: Use scientific facts from the lesson
• Reasoning: Explain how the evidence supports your claim

🫀 Human Digestive System P3 Systems

MOE Learning Objective: Identify the parts of the digestive system and describe their functions in breaking down food.

🎯 By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:

  • Label the main parts: mouth, oesophagus, stomach, small intestine, large intestine
  • Describe the function of each part
  • Explain the path food takes through the body
🇸🇬 Singapore Context: When you eat chicken rice at a hawker centre, your digestive system breaks down the rice (carbohydrates), chicken (protein), and sauce (fats) into nutrients your body can use for energy!

🧠 Check Your Understanding

1. Where does most digestion and absorption of nutrients happen? MCQ

🦋 Life Cycles of Animals P4 Cycles

MOE Learning Objective: Compare the life cycles of animals with 3 stages (e.g., grasshopper) and 4 stages (e.g., butterfly).

🎯 By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:

  • Describe the 4 stages of a butterfly: Egg → Larva (caterpillar) → Pupa → Adult
  • Describe the 3 stages of a grasshopper: Egg → Nymph → Adult
  • Explain why young animals may look different from adults
🇸🇬 Singapore Context: Visit the Butterfly Park at Sentosa or Gardens by the Bay to observe real butterfly life cycles! You can also find grasshoppers in park connectors like the Park Connector Network (PCN).

🧠 Check Your Understanding

1. Which stage is found in a butterfly's life cycle but NOT in a grasshopper's? MCQ

🧊 States of Matter P4 Diversity

MOE Learning Objective: Compare the properties of solids, liquids and gases; understand that matter can change state when heated or cooled.

🎯 By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:

  • Describe properties: solids (fixed shape/volume), liquids (fixed volume, no fixed shape), gases (no fixed shape/volume)
  • Explain melting, freezing, evaporation and condensation
  • Give examples of matter changing state in daily life
🇸🇬 Singapore Context: In our hot, humid climate, you often see condensation on cold drinks (like teh tarik!) and evaporation from puddles after rain. Ice from the freezer melts quickly at room temperature!

🧠 Check Your Understanding

1. Which statement about gases is TRUE? MCQ

💧 The Water Cycle P5 Cycles

MOE Learning Objective: Describe the processes of evaporation, condensation and precipitation in the water cycle.

🎯 By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:

  • Explain how the Sun's heat causes evaporation
  • Describe how water vapour condenses to form clouds
  • Understand how precipitation returns water to Earth
  • Relate the water cycle to Singapore's water management
🇸🇬 Singapore Context: Singapore collects rainwater in reservoirs like Marina Reservoir. Through NEWATER, we also recycle used water! Understanding the water cycle helps us appreciate why water conservation is important in our land-scarce nation.

🧠 Check Your Understanding

1. Explain why puddles on the ground disappear on a hot sunny day in Singapore. Use the terms evaporation and heat in your answer. CER

CER Structure:
• Claim: The puddles disappear because...
• Evidence: Heat from the Sun causes...
• Reasoning: This shows that...

⚡ Electrical Systems P5 Systems Energy

MOE Learning Objective: Draw and interpret circuit diagrams; identify conductors and insulators; understand that a complete circuit is needed for current to flow.

🎯 By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:

  • Draw simple circuit diagrams using standard symbols
  • Explain why a circuit must be complete for the bulb to light up
  • Test materials to classify them as conductors or insulators
  • Describe safe use of electricity at home
🇸🇬 Singapore Context: Singapore uses a 230V electrical system. Always use 3-pin plugs with the earth wire for safety! SP Group manages our electricity supply, and we can save energy by switching off lights and fans when not in use.

🧠 Check Your Understanding

1. Which material is a GOOD conductor of electricity? MCQ

🎯 Forces P6 Interactions

MOE Learning Objective: Identify different types of forces (friction, gravity, elastic); explain how forces affect motion; apply concepts to real-world situations.

🎯 By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:

  • Define force as a push or pull
  • Compare friction, gravity and elastic forces
  • Explain how friction can be useful or a disadvantage
  • Predict how changing forces affects motion
🇸🇬 Singapore Context: Friction helps your shoes grip the floor so you don't slip! At Universal Studios Singapore, roller coasters use gravity to create thrilling drops. Elastic forces are used in the bungee jump at AJ Hackett Sentosa.

🧠 Check Your Understanding

1. A cyclist is riding on a smooth road. Explain why it is harder to cycle on a rough, sandy road. Use the term friction in your answer. CER

CER Structure:
• Claim: It is harder because...
• Evidence: Friction is a force that...
• Reasoning: On a sandy road, friction is...

🕸️ Food Webs & Interactions P6 Interactions

MOE Learning Objective: Construct and interpret food chains and food webs; identify producers, consumers and decomposers; explain how changes in one population affect others.

🎯 By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:

  • Draw food chains using arrows to show energy flow
  • Identify producers (plants), consumers (animals), decomposers
  • Predict effects of removing one organism from a food web
  • Relate to conservation efforts in Singapore
🇸🇬 Singapore Context: In Singapore's nature reserves like Bukit Timah, food webs connect plants, insects, birds, and mammals. NParks works to protect habitats so these interactions remain balanced. Even in our urban environment, we can support biodiversity!

🧠 Check Your Understanding

1. In a food chain, what do the arrows represent? MCQ
